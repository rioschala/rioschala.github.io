In this current society, these words might be already familiar to you. Each Large Language Model (LLM) shows the steps required for completing the user prompt and that, somehow, got engraived into in our brains. Not only has the use of AI technologies been widely approved, promoted and congratulated, but it also has changed how we understand, feel and live ouw world [^1]. 

This new trend, thus, impacts all possible fields of human interaction. Users misuse it, and they are currently finding love, therapists or supporters to ideas that otherwise would have collected zero fans. Yet, that is not the main problem. The main discussion in how easy-to-get-frustrated people have become. Currently, you can see on social media how people reac when big companies modify the way the agents work or reply. To exemplify, the introduction of GPT-5 broke the way it replied and many people felt as if they had lost one friend along the way and demanted to be brought back [^2]. 

Being so attached to a non-sentient, omnipresent and agile machine caused users to lost their ability to either feel  vulnerable or without confidence on several fields. This is only reinforced by the speed these models solve the prompts and create a false ilusion of knowledge, completition and accomplishment.
